# TradingView Idea Template — Blueprint Trading System

> **Purpose**: Use this template to generate professional TradingView idea posts that build account credibility through accurate forecasts. Each idea follows the full Seven-Step Decision Process.

---

## POSTING STRATEGY FOR CREDIBILITY

### Goals
- Post 3-5 ideas per week across different asset classes
- Focus on HIGH-CONVICTION setups only (composite score >= 6.0)
- Track win rate publicly — aim for 60%+ with 1:2+ average R:R
- Use educational tone — explain WHY, not just entry/exit
- Build a track record over 3-6 months before monetizing

### What Makes a Credible Post
1. Clear directional bias with reasoning (not "I think it will go up")
2. Specific entry, stop, and target levels with R:R calculation
3. Multi-timeframe analysis shown (HTF context + LTF execution)
4. Fundamental backing (COT, Valuation, Seasonality)
5. Follow-up updates when targets hit or stops triggered
6. Honest about losses — show full track record

## DO's AND DON'Ts

### DO
- Show your full analysis with reasoning
- Include specific price levels (entry, stop, targets)
- Post follow-ups on EVERY idea (wins AND losses)
- Use educational language ("this zone shows...", "the methodology suggests...")
- Credit the methodology ("supply/demand zone analysis")
- Track and display your running statistics

### DON'T
- Never say "guaranteed" or "100% sure"
- Never post without stop loss levels
- Never skip the risk disclaimer
- Never delete losing ideas (transparency builds trust)
- Never post ideas at equilibrium (no edge = no post)
- Never rush — quality over quantity
- Never provide specific financial advice ("you should buy...")

### LANGUAGE GUIDE
Instead of: "Buy EUR/USD now!"
Say: "EUR/USD showing a high-quality demand zone setup with bullish fundamental alignment"

Instead of: "This trade will hit target"
Say: "The methodology suggests favorable conditions for this setup with 1:3 R:R potential"

Instead of: "I'm certain about this"
Say: "5/5 bias factors aligned — highest conviction setup in our methodology"
